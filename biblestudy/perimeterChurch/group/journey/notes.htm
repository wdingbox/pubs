“…yet for us there is one God, the Father, from whom are all things and for whom we exist, and one Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom are all things and through whom we exist.” 1 CORINTHIANS 8:6 (ESV)
elicits a yawn from Christians
because they one of the Bible’s paradoxical truths: the tri-unity (or, threein-oneness) of God
 
There is only one God. (Genesis 1:1; Deuteronomy 6:4; Isaiah 44:6)
 
• The Father is God. (John 6:27; 1 Corinthians 8:6)
• The Son is God. (John 1:18, 5:18; 10:30-33)
• The Holy Spirit is God. (Matthew 3:16; Matthew 28:19)
 
• The Father is not the Son. (John 6:46; John 13:3)
• The Son is not the Holy Spirit. (Luke 1:35; John 3:34)
• The Father is not the Holy Spirit. (Matthew 3:16; Ephesians 2:18)
