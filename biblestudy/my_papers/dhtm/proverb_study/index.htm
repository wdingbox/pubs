wisdom ( ˈwɪzdəm) noun

Wordly Definition

  1. the ability or result of an ability to think and act utilizing knowledge, experience, understanding, common sense, and insight
  2. accumulated knowledge, erudition, or enlightenment
  3. archaic a wise saying or wise sayings or teachings
  4. obsolete soundness of mind [Old English wīsdōm; see wise1, -dom]

Hebrew

חָכְמָה hok-marw, feminine noun, x149

  1. wisdom,
  2. skill (in war)
  3. wisdom (in administration)
  4. shrewdness, wisdom
  5. wisdom, prudence (in religious affairs)
  6. wisdom (ethical and religious)

Notes:

  1. first appeared in Exodus Exo 28:3
  2. Most frequently used in Proverb (x39), Ecc (x28) and Job (x18).
  3. root, become wise.

Biblical Definition

  1. about the knowledge or understanding of the Holy One.

  2. the beginning of wisdom (or the bases of wisdom, the foundation of the wisdom) is the fear of the LORD.
  3. the ending of wisdom (the ultimate purpose of wisdom) is to abide, serve and love the LORD.
  4. wisdom is a tool from the Holy One for children of God to live in the world.
