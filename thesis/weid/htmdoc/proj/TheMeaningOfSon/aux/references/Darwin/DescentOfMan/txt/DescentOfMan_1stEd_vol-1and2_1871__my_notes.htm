The sole object of this work is to consider, firstly, whether man, like every other species, is descended from some pre-existing form;

secondly, the manner of  his development; and

thirdly, the value of the differences between the so-called races of man.

It has often and confidently been asserted, that man's origin can never be known: but ignorance more frequently begets confidence than does knowledge: it is those who know little, and not those who know much, who so positively assert that this or that problem will never be solved by science. The conclusion that man is the co-descendant with other species of some ancient, lower, and extinct form, is not in any degree new.
