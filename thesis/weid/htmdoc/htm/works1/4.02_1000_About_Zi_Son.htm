About Chinese Character Zi (the Son)

 

1. Introduction

As compared with the Chinese Cross Character (CCC), the meaning for the Chinese character Zi seemed clear and simple in history -- it means the son, the boy or the male child and used until today. This character frequently appeared in Chinese classical books, especialy during the Autum and Spring Dynasty (around 500 BC), in person's name -- called Zhu-Zi (ZZ) phenonmenon.

Some scholars has noticed the specilities about the Zi in TBI studies. However, the original intention and the purpose of the creation of Zi in Chinese writing system was never fully clarified so far.

 

2. Gender Asymmetric Phenomenon (GAP)

It is common in real world that two things that are same kind of, but both depend on and contradict to each other to form a set. Such kind of two different but related things are usually called a pair, or a couple. All related paired things can be arranged in a definite pattern in a table in form of corresponding parts. Expressed in more math way, it is called symmetric -- when table flips or rotates, the cell can be matched in a pair. Following is an example of symmetric table for paired characters. For TBI, we can find that some characters (1,2,3) are in pair of both in concepts, writings and usages.

See (*).

 

For human being who have same kind of social status, ages or family relationship etc., but with different genders can form a symmetric table for specific languages. For example, in English, there exists individual paired words for each type of persons -- man vs woman, husband vs wife, father vs mother, son vs daughter, boy vs girl, brother vs sister, king vs queen, etc.

However, in Chinese writing system, there are no paired individual characters for son, boy and king, etc. Instead, they use phrases, like, female son, female boy or female king etc. to express the daughter, girl or queen respectively. Following table listed all paired characters in TBI. Obviously in TBI, they are not symmetric. We call this phenonmenon as Gender Asymmetric Phenomenom (GAP) in TBI.

See (*).

 

From OOA viewpoints, the characters population methods are completely different between the different genders. All characters that represent females were distinguished by the single root pictogram that abstracted from the observation of the image of weman. Whereas for male characters, they were created through aggregation of the root logographs, like cross, nail, hand, rod, king, etc. The distinction is so sharp that they are not compariable to each other. They were generated from different worlds at inital design time. Since very begining, the concepts for persons were asymmetric in Chinse writing language.

Even characters in meaning belong to the the pair of genders, they were not used together in pair in any compound characters in TBI. There were no happy families -- husband-wife, brothers-sisters, or father-mother-son etc. What most frequently appeared in pair in compund character were the 'son' and 'woman', and it means good and remained until today [WordFrequenciesInGAP].

The GAP is one of fundmental unique features differentiated from any alphabetic phonetic languages. and the alpabetta languages. However, such difference is not noticable during the litures translation.
