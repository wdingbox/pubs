About Factoring

The objective of the factoring process is to extract the root element among about 6,000 distinct compound characters. The TBI factoring process is very similar to the calculation that is used in algebra. There are two steps:

  1. Find out the GCF(greatest Common Factor) by visual comparison of multiple words. There are no effective automatic digital reconizer in the world that can help us right now.
  2. Once the GCF is found out, disassembly the word with GCF.

Once we finished the factoring process, we get about 1,000 root characters.

For example:   for three words ABCDEF, ABMNQ, ABXYZ,  their GCF is AB. AB can further found in other words, AU, AV, or BM,BW, so A and B are GCF. Hence, ABCDEF can be expressed as:  (ABCDEF) = (A )+ (B) + (CDEF).

Example Of Factoring
  Words Factored Words

7 BW (B)+(W)
