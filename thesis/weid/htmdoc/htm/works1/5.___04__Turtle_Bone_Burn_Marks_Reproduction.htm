Burn Mark Reproduction

Objective:

  1. Reproduce the burn mark through buring inceses on plastron.
  2. Compare the reproduced incense burn mark with unearthed real one.
  3. Compare the incense burn mark with metal rod burn mark.

Tools:

  1. electrical drills (e.g.  Dremel200, to make ditch, shallow hollows )
  2. spoons (to heat pine resin)
  3. electric oven (to melt pine resin by heat)
  4. knife (cut wood braces)
  5. saw (cut wood braces, turtle bones etc)
  6. cigeritte lighter (light incense)
  7. iron rod (1 cm in diameter)

Contrast Incense Burn Marks With Unearthed:

Contrasted the incense burn mark to the unearthed ones, we can find common features on its surface:

  1. narrow long ditch looks like a firewall -- one side is burnt, the other is not.
  2. the burn marks are alway solid carbon black.
  3. there is a circular yellow scortch around black carbon spot -- caused by incense or pine-resin.

Conclusion:

  1. The incense burn marks are more alike to the unearthed in apparence than the metal rod.
  2. The narrow long ditch is used to fix wood chip brace to allow incense to lean on.
  3. The shallow hollow was used to help to erect incense.
  4. No cracks were found after burn.
  5. The broken sound (like pop) can be heard for several times during the cooling process after incense burnt out.
