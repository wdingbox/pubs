1.2. The Meaning of 'Pregnant'

As compared with the generation process from 'man' to 'pregnant', there is a  similar way for woman. But was not used to represent the 'pregnant' today. Instead,  it used the "Chen" and "work" associated with 'woman' of two different characters to denote the meaning of the 'pregnamt' --  both of them are still in use today.

The character 'work' was made of two horizontal lines connected by a viertial line -- it could mean the connection and the road between heaven and earth.

Obviously they did this on purpose to indicate the character 'son' was to mean the "son of man".Obviously such stop was on purpose.

Chen analysis show that it was related to stable and manger.

1.3. The raise of son

The 'son' frequently associated with womon[WordFrequenciesInGAP] with various type of characters like, 'good', 'raise' and many unknown for today like which related to manger etc.

 

From the structure and meaning of 'pregnant', we can concluded that the 'son' in TBI was not just the biological boy. He was a special son-of-man associated with the 'stables' and 'work' between heaven and earth -- which showed the idential features to what the Gospel stated.

 

2. Gender Asymmetric Phenomenon (GAP)

There is an gender asymmetric phenomenom (GAP) in TBI -- it means the roles and features are asymmetric between the characters of gender related. The pair of femal and human (not male) used to generate 'pragnant' character is a typical example. It did not mean social inequality between male and female.  It showed us that the 'son' in the TBI was not as the biological boy as what we normally thought in daily life.

We listed all characters that represent the people in TBI. As compared with the English language, all words are in pair -- man vs woman, husband vs wife, father vs mother, son vs doughter, boy vs girl, brother vs sister, king vs queen. But it is not the case for Chinese -- there is a GAP.

The GAP started during the inital design time. From OOA viewpoints, all female characters were inherited from only one base pictograph. Whereas for male characters, with GCF method, they use the root logographs, like cross, nail, hand, rod, king, field, plow, etc. to compose the characters.  There is no any opposite feature between characters of males and females .

Even characters belong to the the pair of genders, they did not appear together in pair in any compound characters in TBI. What most frequently appeared and only remained until today in pair in compund character were the 'son' and 'woman' in character 'good'.

Among all friends of 'woman', 'son' has highest frequency. Among all friends of 'son', 'woman' has highest frequency. So 'son' and 'woman' are mutal best freinds in TBI.

The character 'good' is used every day in our daily life today. However, the root reason that 'son' and 'woman' together were to mean the 'good' was lost in history[ShuoWenHao]. As matter of factes, the 'good' that was presented by 'son' and 'woman', not only can be found in Chinese characters, but also in Egypt Hierogliphics[TBIAndHierogplyphicsForGood], as well as the oil paintings in Europe, especially during the Renaissance. (See samples of oil paintings[OilPaintingGood])

The GAP in TBI show that there is no geneology relationship or any other common ground among them. However, all of these unrelated characters can only be perfectly and organically linked together through the Gospel account -- the virgin Mary, the son of man, the brothers, the master, the heavenly father, the King, etc. -- all of which was to mean a 'good' message.
