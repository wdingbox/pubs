2. The Son's Family
 

By collecting all root characters together that are similar to the 'son' not only in image but also in meaning, with OOA modeling method, we built a big family for 'son'. (See following table).

Among the children of 'son', there is a step son -- it visually looks like a manger, and it has no inherited featured from parent's image, but it occupied the first position of the 'son' among the twelve earthly-stems. (See table)

The 'manger' has a child called 'Zhen', it is the highest frequency character of the TBI. By applying the statistic theory -- what the most frequently used 'Zhen' represented is what the whole TBI implied, also, through the OOA modeling -- 'Zhen' is the grand child of 'son', we can conclude that the central topic of the whole TBI is regarding about the message related to the 'son'.

The character of 'manger' in TBI has been fogotten in history. But its children Zhen(贞) and Din(鼎) were actively used in classical books. Today the scholars are still arguing for the confusion existed between them[ZhangJingYu]. In Chinese dictionay, the Zhen could means the 'chastity', 'pure', 'virtuous', 'incorruptible','dedication', 'devotion'. Din reprents the bronze infering the 'King', 'ruler', 'power', 'mighty'. Including the manger itself, all of them were perfectly consistent to the story the Gospels.

In family of 'son', there were three characters used in heaven-stems and earth-stems. (See table of counting system)  This is the evidence demonstrated that the 'son' were closely related to heaven, earth and the laws of universe which is what the Gospel meaned to.

Through GCF analysis of the son's family, its results show that the featured root characters contains the information like crown of throne, hang, raise-up, bent, pressure etc. that enriched the meaning of the 'son' in TBI that can be linked to Gospel account. (see table below)

From OOA viewpoints, we can find that the family of 'son' is the biggest one among the children of 'seven'. Nearly 7% of all root characters belongs to the children of 'son'. Nearly 4% compound words are composed of the son's family.
