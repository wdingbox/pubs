Chinese Language Special Features:

  1. Dictionary free:

    The encapsulation of meaning and image in chinese word make it Chinese language dictionary free. The real world itself is the dictionary of chinese words. If people forgot the meaning of the word, people can get it back by compare the word to the object in world to get it back.
    Chinese character writing, its meanings, visual thinking and religious thoughts are undividable.

    On the contary, in alphabetic language, people can not get the meaning without the its dictionary. Rossetta stone is a sample.

    The earliest dictionary in chinese history that was called ShuoWen (600AD) was really not necessary and indeed it produced lots of errors and misleading.
     
  2. Grammar free:

    Since the image and meaning are encapsulated in word, the sequence of words is the sequence of image, which become a vivid moving picture - vedio. So in Chinese writing, there are no significant differences between the word and article. Everything is images or symbols. No grammar is needed.


    The earliest Chinese grammar book was written in English by Robert Morrison[RobertMorrison] in 1815. Later on, the Chinese scholar Ma JianZhong[MaJianZhong] published the book of Wen Tong 文通 in 1898, that was the first book about Chinese grammar writen in Chinese. 
     
  3. Punctuation free

    There is no any punctuation marks in TBI. You can read chinese as if you were watch the cartoons movies. Each character is a picture drawing, each picture is worth thousand of words. In TBI chinese, Each character's meaning is not strongly affected by contexts.
     
  4. Pronounciation Free

    No pronounciation is need for each single character. On one hand, any people can associate any pronounciation on the word. It is best recommended international writing system.

    On the other hand, pronounciation free cause multiple dialects in China and make it difficult in oral communication. Currently it can be roughly classified into the seven large groups,  Putonghua (Mandarin), Gan, Kejia (Hakka), Min, Wu, Xiang and Yue (Cantonese), taking up about 92% of total populations.
     
  5. Style free

    The meaning of the word used in the book or texts can be changed as times and history changes. (word slips)  The encapsulation of image and meaning in chinese word ensure that the meanings of the word, visual thinking and religious thoughts are undividiable and never changes, even the forms of word is changed. This feature further help the chinees language as dictiory free.

    On the other hand, the encapsulation make it possible that the meaning of the words used in the text can be different from the meaning of the words itself.

    As compared with alphabetic language, where the meaning or defination of each word can be arbitary changed, the original meaning of the word could slip away. The original meaning could be tatally lost. (Rosetta)
     
  6. Fast reading

    Chinese word is difficult to write and no clude for pronounciation. But it is easy to read since the form of word, a readily recognizable picture or symbols, can explains everything. Ideographic conveying their visual message directly to our minds, thus bypassing the restrictive intermediary of speech, with human visual thinking, ideas can immediately pop into human minds when seeing them, just like a road stop sign.
