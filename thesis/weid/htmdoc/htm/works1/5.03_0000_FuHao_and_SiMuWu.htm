1. Introduction

Chinese archeologists and scholars usually use the inscriptures on bronzes discovered on its site as primary hard evidences to identify the owner or the nature of its tomb -- even no soft evidences could be found. Typical examples of bronzes are the Fu-Hao at tomb site #5 and Si-Mu-Wu in royal grave garden. This methodology is not adquate for the tombs found in Anyang.

 

2. Fu-Hao

See (*)

The so-called Tomb of Fu-Hao (#5) was discovered in 1976 at temples areas of Anyang city. The excavation was officially conducted by the Anyang Working Team of the Archaeological Institute of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences. It is the only original archaeological site at Anyan areas without any destruction or robery. Among 1928 different unearthed materials (https://depts.washington.edu/chinaciv/archae/2fuhmain.htm), there are 468 bronze objects, 209 of them contains inscriptions, 109 of them with inscriptons of 'Fu-Hao'. Due this reason, the site was widely considered as the tomb for lady Fu-Hao -- a queen of King Wu Ding.

However, some scholars questioned the nature of the excation site(#5). Zhang Sufeng etc. provided strong solid avidences to challenge the current conclusion[ZhangSufengBuShiXia].

  1. The site#5 was the only tomb in the worship temples areas. It was not inside of the rolyal graves garden.
  2. If we considter site #5 as a tomb, its rectangle structure was too simple as compared with other tombes in the graves garden.
  3. As the only well deseved tomb found in Yinxu, no formal coffin and body of owner was ever discovered. It is not like a tombs at all.
  4. Among 190 bronzes with inscriptions, there exist at least nine different inscriptions other than Fu-Hao. For example, like SiMuKui, YaQi etc.

The critical issue to interpret this picture lie in the confusion in meaning of character of 'Zhou' 帚. 3. Si-Mu-Wu

picture

Si-Mu-Wu is the largest and heaviest bronze ware ever discovered in Chinese history. The tetripod is 133 centimetres high, 110 centimetres wide, and 79 centimetres deep and weighs 832.84 kilograms(1,925 pounds). It is of the rectangular type, with a squared body and four legs. It was unearthed in Wuguan Village, Anyang, Henan in 1939. Now it is stored in The National Museum of China in Beijing.

Based on its inscription of Si-Mu-Wu(司母戊) that was found on the interior wall, this utensil was widely considered to be build for the monther 'Wu'. Accordingly, the excavation site where this bonze was unearthed was considered as the tomb belonging to the same femal person -- the Queen, or the step-monter of the King.

Since the 1970s there existed an augument between the interprestion of 司 and  后 among archaeologists and scholars. Finally The National Museum of China has officially changed the name of the bronze from 司 to 后. After 2011, Chinese TV began to use 后母戊 for broadcast in public. This led to the bronze has another meaning -- step-mother.

As matter of fact, the augument between the Si and Hou is a misleading for all of us. There is no difference between Si and Hou in TBI. The original meaning of Si or Hou has already totally lost in history.

If we consider all characters on the bronze as one single harmony integrated picture, it is not hard for us to clearly see that there is a woman, along with a cross and weapon in it. With BE anaylysis method, if we link the weapon as 'killing' or macrasss, these three features can closely match the story accounted in Gospel.  Futher with BE method, using visiual thinking, we can recongnized that Si as picture to represent a stable and manger.

The soft evidence we can find in classic books, is the compound family name Si-Ma. The famous ancient historian's name Si-Ma-Qian(司马迁) is the well-known example.  This compund name is still in use for today. It clearly tell us the Si and horse are closely related -- just like that the horse and stable are naturally always in one picture.  

From OOA viewpoints, Si is a compound character that composed of a manger (ImageOfMangerInMegiddo) and a building of shelter. With polymorphism, the character 'manger' also looks like a mouth of baby to represent 'speaking'. Throughout TBI, two characters are similar to 'manger' -- Qi and Chi (teeth). We can consider them as the variation of manger. Visually Chi is more similar to mouth in image, but it was intentionally not used to represent the 'mouth' or 'speak' at all (MouthAndTeeth). Because Chi could not visually represent the manger at the same time. It lost the OOA polymorphism feature, and became a pictorgraph to represent the limited meaning for 'teeth'.

4. Synthesis Analysis

Although Fu-Hao and Si-Mu-Wu came from different bronzes and different archeological site with different periods at TBI times, with BE analysis method, we can find that they refer to a shared common meaning -- 'woman', 'cross', 'heaven', 'son'

The characters of 'stable' and 'manger', 'women', 'comet', 'son', 'cross' that we discussed here at TBI times can commonly found in bronzes in other sites and other period of times in history. To consider the characters on bronzes as names of the owners has brought confusions in archeological study. For example, in Fu-Hao tombe, there were bronzes with inscriptions of 'Ya-Qi', 'Si-Mu' etc.

Throughout all unearthed materials at Anyang, no personal glorified idols were found. If the person, no matter who he was a king or queen, were not the center of worship, then the person's name was not necessary needed to be specially recorded.

The BBO phenonmen also told us that the characters at TBI times were considered as sacred same as the bronzes. Characters were only used for worship purpose. So characters were less likely to be used for worshippers.

5. Broze Inscription (BI) Analysis

Collecting all Bronzes Inscriptions (BI), we can find that there about only 300 different characters used on bronzes. By applying usage frequency (UF) statistic method, we can turn out that the top three most frequently used characters are 'Fu' (father), 'Y' (cross), and 'Zhen' (son).  'Fu', 'Hao', 'Si' we disscussed are among the top 30 characters.

If we compare BI UF with TBI RF, we can find that they are significaly close in Zipf's curve line. Compared with  unearthed materials of TBI that most of them were broken into pieces, BI perfectly preserved the whole contents of BI characters although the total number of characters we found were less than TBI.
